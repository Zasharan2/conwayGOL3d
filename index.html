Conway's Game of Life 3D

By Zasharan2

In this project, I coded a 3D extension to the classic Conway's Game of Life (GOL).

Here's how it works:
When the project loads, you are greeted with a beautiful purple (magenta?) title screen.
The settings button allows you to choose the number of dimensions. This is by default 2.

The 2D GOL rules remain the same:
1) A living cell continues to the next generation if and only if it has 2 or 3 live cells next to it, otherwise it dies.
2) A dead cell becomes live if it has exactly 3 live cells next to it.
Being "next to" a cell in 2D simply means to be one of the 8 cells that are immediately adjacent or diagonal a cell.

In 3D GOL, the rules are:
1) A living cell continues to the next generation if and only if it has 9, 10, or 11 live cells next to it, otherwise it dies.
2) A dead cell becomes live if it has exactly 10 live cells next to it.
Being "next to" a cell in 3D simply means to be one of the 26 cells that are immediately adjacent or diagonal a cell.

When playing, left click a tile to turn it live, and right click to kill it.
In 3D GOL, there are indicators on the left and right as for what z-dimension you are in.
Navigate the z-dimension with up and down arrow keys, or with the W and S keys.
Press space to advance to the next generation.

And that's it! Have fun making cool stuff in 3D GOL! (Board size currently limited to 20x20x20, might change)
